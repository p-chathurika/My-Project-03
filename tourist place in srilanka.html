Srilanka is an island country located off the south coast of India.
It is known for its diverse wildlife, beautiful beaches, and ancient ruins.
Here are some of the must-visit tourist places in Srilanka.


1. Sigiriya


Sigiriya is a fifth century rock fortress located in the Matale district of Srilanka.
It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its beautiful frescoes and ancient gardens.
Sigiriya is sometimes called the "Eighth Wonder of the World."
The name refers to a site of historical and archaeological significance that is dominated by a massive column of rock nearly 200 metres (660 ft) high.
According to local tradition, this site was selected by King Kasyapa (477 – 495 CE) for his new capital.
He built his palace on the top of this rock and decorated its sides with colorful frescoes.
The capital and the royal palace was abandoned after the king's death.
It was used as a Buddhist monastery until the 14th century.
Sigiriya today is a UNESCO listed World Heritage Site.
It is one of the best preserved examples of ancient urban planning.


2. The Temple of the Tooth Relic

The Temple of the Tooth Relic is a Buddhist temple in the city of Kandy, Sri Lanka.
It is one of the most sacred temples in the country, as it houses the relic of the tooth of the Buddha.
The relic has played an important role in local politics since ancient times and it is considered a symbol of sovereignty.
The temple is part of the World Heritage Site of the Sacred City of Kandy.
The temple was built in the 16th century by the Kingdom of Kandy, and it is believed to be built over the site of a previous temple.
The temple complex is made up of four main buildings.
The first is the outer wall which encompasses the entire complex.
The second is the main shrine, which houses the relic of the tooth. The third is the Paththirippuwa or Octagon, which is a pavilion in the middle of the complex.
The fourth is the Natha Devale, which is a shrine dedicated to the guardian deity of the relic.
The temple is a popular destination for pilgrims and tourists alike.
Apart from the relic, the temple complex also houses a museum, which contains various artifacts related to the history and culture of Sri Lanka.
There are also a number of other shrines and temples within the complex


3. Adam's Peak

Adam's Peak is a mountain located in the central highlands of Srilanka.
It is a popular pilgrimage site for Buddhists, Hindus, Muslims, and Christians alike.
The peak of the mountain is said to have a footprint of Lord Buddha.
Adam's Peak is a 2,243-meter tall mountain located in the central highlands of Sri Lanka.
It is a popular pilgrimage destination, particularly for Buddhists and Hindus, who believe that a footprint on the summit of the mountain is that of the Buddha.
The peak is also known as Sri Pada, which means “sacred footprint” in Sinhala.
The peak is a popular tourist attraction and the surrounding area has been declared a national park.
It is also home to a variety of wildlife such as endemic species of birds, monkeys, and leopards.
The climb to the summit can be a challenging but rewarding experience, with breathtaking views and a unique cultural experience.


4. Yala National Park

Yala National Park is a wildlife reserve located in the south-eastern coast of Srilanka.
It is home to a variety of wildlife including elephants, leopards, and crocodiles.
It is also known for its beautiful beaches and scenic views.
Yala National Park is the most visited and second largest national park in Sri Lanka.
It covers an area of 979 square kilometres (378 sq mi) and is located in the southeast region of the country, bordering the Indian Ocean.
The park is best known for its variety of wild animals, including elephants, leopards, sloth bears, crocodiles, and over 215 species of birds.
Yala is also home to a number of archaeological sites, including ancient Buddhist temples and ruins.


5. Anuradhapura

Anuradhapura is an ancient city located in the North Central Province of Srilanka.
It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is home to a number of ancient ruins and monuments.
Anuradhapura is a city in Sri Lanka that was once the capital of the ancient Sinhalese civilization. The city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is home to numerous ancient monuments, temples and ruins, many of which are still in use today. It is a popular tourist destination and is known for its religious and cultural significance.
The city is also home to a number of important archaeological sites, including the ancient city of Mihintale, the Jetavanarama temple, the Thuparama dagoba, the Isurumuniya Vihara temple, the Kuttam Pokuna, and the Ruwanwelisaya dagoba.


6. Galle Fort

Galle Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most important landmarks in Sri Lanka.
Galle Fort, located in Sri Lanka, is a fortification first built by the Portuguese in 1588 and then extensively fortified by the Dutch in the 17th century.
The Galle Fort was declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1988, and is the largest remaining fortress in Asia built by European occupiers.
It is a historic, cultural, and architectural landmark, and is one of the most important tourist attractions in Sri Lanka.
The fort consists of a walled city, built on a promontory that overlooks the Indian Ocean.
The walls have four main gates: the Zwart (Black) Gate, the Middel (Middle) Gate, the Sun (Sea) Gate, and the Lipton’s Seat Gate.
Inside the fort, there are numerous churches, museums, and other buildings of interest.
The fort is also home to the National Maritime Museum, which is dedicated to the maritime history of Sri Lanka.


7.Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya is a city in the hill country of Sri Lanka, known for its tea plantations.
Nuwara Eliya is a hill station in the Central Province of Sri Lanka.
It is located at an altitude of 1,868 m (6,128 ft) and is considered to be the most important location in the country for tea production.
The city was founded by the British in 1846 and became a popular tourist destination for its natural beauty, cool climate and its colonial architecture.
Nuwara Eliya is home to many attractions including the Hakgala Botanical Garden, the Lake Gregory, the Victoria Park, and Sita Amman Temple. It is also known for its many tea plantations, waterfalls and hiking trails.
